I am an M.S. student in Statistics at Seoul National University, advised by Professor Won Chang. My research interests are in statistical methodology for valid black-box-assisted inference: methods that use pretrained models, scientific simulators, neural surrogates, or synthetic data to improve inference while preserving calibrated uncertainty under model misspecification and distribution shift.

Before joining the Statistics program, I received a B.S. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from Seoul National University. My background combines statistical inference, probability, machine learning, and scientific computation.

Research interests

I am interested in developing statistically valid and computationally practical inference methods for settings where limited target-domain data are supplemented by imperfect external models, including pretrained predictors, scientific simulators, neural surrogates, amortized posterior approximations, and synthetic data generators.

  • Conformal and distribution-free inference
  • Prediction-powered and black-box-assisted inference
  • Calibrated Bayesian and simulation-based inference
  • Uncertainty quantification for scientific simulators and neural surrogates
  • Inference under model misspecification and distribution shift
